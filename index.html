Hands-on UX, in the pub.

UXup is a practitioner-led learning community in Brighton. Each event picks one theme — discovery, content design, research methods — and we work through it together: a short talk, a hands-on exercise, then a drink.

What happens at a UXup

Short talk

One practitioner shares a real piece of work — discovery that went off-rails, a research method that paid off, a content-design decision under pressure. ~20 minutes.

Hands-on workshop

We pull out the laptops (or paper, or post-its) and actually try the thing. Bring something real you're working on — feedback from the room afterwards.

A drink + chat

The bit where the actual community happens. Stay for one, stay for three. Conversations carry on long after the slides close.

Who comes along

Designers + researchers building practice

Mid-career folks looking to sharpen specific skills, steal methods that work, and find people who get the weird ones too.

Senior practitioners with stories

Designers and researchers who want to teach as well as learn. Run a workshop. Bring a problem from your week and get the room thinking about it with you.

Adjacent roles

Product managers, devs, content designers, anyone who works alongside UX and wants to do it more thoughtfully. No prerequisites beyond curiosity.
